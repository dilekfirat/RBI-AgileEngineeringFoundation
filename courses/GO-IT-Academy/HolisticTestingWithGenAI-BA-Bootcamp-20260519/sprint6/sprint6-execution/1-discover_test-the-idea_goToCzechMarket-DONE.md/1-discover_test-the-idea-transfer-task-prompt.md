# **Title**

Hands-on: Czech-market expansion idea—structured discovery (you write the prompts)


# **Background**

The **ToolShop** is the system under test. A new investor wants customer-base growth; the roadmap order under discussion is Czech Republic first, then United States (East Coast), then China. This exercise focuses on **testing the idea** of Czech expansion: a structured market evaluation grounded in **business evidence** from public sources (competitors, reviews, pricing, ecommerce trends, Czech market data). This handout is intentionally **not** a 1:1 prompt for a model: you choose goals, scope, output shape, citation expectations, and follow-up questions yourself.

# **Tasks**

1. Read the transfer task objective and investor context; list **your** open questions and success criteria for what “good evidence” means before you prompt any model.

2. Author **prompt A** for one model/provider, aimed at producing a **markdown** evaluation that supports product, marketing, and investment discussion—not a generic essay. You decide structure, depth, and how you ask for citations or uncertainty.

3. OPTIONAL: Author **prompt B** for a **different** model or provider (or a clearly different instruction style); avoid pasting one mega-prompt unchanged—adapt to learn what changes coverage and quality.

4. Ensure your investigation and consolidated artefact address: **target customer groups** in the Czech market for ToolShop; **at least three** competitors or alternatives with **comparable** treatment of positioning, pricing, assortment, and customer experience; **needs, pains, and buying expectations**; **legal, language, logistics, payment, and localization** considerations; **opportunities and risks**; and a **recommendation** (expand, pilot, or defer) with **explicit evidence and assumptions**.

5. Consolidate into **your** markdown file (sections and tables you judge fit for a sprint review).

6. Submit **your prompt(s)** as used, the markdown artefact, and a **short reflection** on prompt design (what worked, hallucination or weak-evidence risks, language of sources, format) and what you would change next time.

7. Cross-check a subset of conclusions against **observable ToolShop** facts (e.g. current shipping, payment, language, or catalogue signals from the product or official materials) and note **alignments or gaps** between the market idea and today’s product reality.
