# Black story (variant: discover / test the idea — geography & evidence)

Same sprint pattern as `1-discover_test-the-idea-transfer-task-prompt.md` (ToolShop, investor pressure, evidence-led market evaluation); scenario below is **Slovakia entry** with a **human typo + model “fix”** failure mode.

---

## Title

Clean Spellcheck, Wrong Border

---

## Difficulty

Medium

---

## Teaser

- ToolShop’s Board wanted an evidence-backed plan to enter **Slovakia**, and the team published a long markdown analysis—competitors, logistics, payments, regulation—with citations that passed spot checks.
- The assistant had quietly rewritten the sales sponsor’s garbled intake wording so the brief read cleanly before the first research sentence, and the pack never pinned an ISO country next to the narrative everyone thought they were reading.
- The investor approved heavy spend from that document, yet after go-live the operation bled cash while shoppers and shelf talk kept routing demand to **Amazon’s** dominant tool-buying path in the territory those pages had actually described.

---

## Hidden Solution

- The sponsor had typed “**Slovenska market**” (a slip between English and Slovak phrasing around **Slovakia**), and the model “corrected” the token toward the **Slovenian** retail context—so every table, law pointer, and rival URL tracked **Slovenia**, not **Slovakia**.
- The deck stayed self-consistent and footnoted, so reviewers treated the polished language as proof of intent instead of running a hard geography gate against the original one-liner.
- ToolShop therefore executed a strategy that was sound **for the wrong country**, where **Amazon’s tool-shop-style proposition** was already entrenched, while the Slovak opportunity was never modeled—burn followed from the mismatch, not from random bad luck.
