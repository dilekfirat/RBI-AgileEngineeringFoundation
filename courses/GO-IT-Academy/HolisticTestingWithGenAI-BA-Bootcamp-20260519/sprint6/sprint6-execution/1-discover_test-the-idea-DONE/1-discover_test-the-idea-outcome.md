## 3. Answer to the business question

**Question (one sentence):** For a European **specialized tools / workshop** webshop (the Sprint 6 “toolShop” narrative), does **entering Czechia** make enough business sense to fund a **pilot**, given price pressure and localization cost?

**Verdict: Yes — as a bounded marketplace-led pilot, not as an immediate “full replatform + brand blitz.”**

**Why (grounded in the signals above):**

1. **Demand pool + category fit:** Czech online retail is **multi‑billion USD** and growing (S1, S9), and **Hobby & Leisure** is the **top revenue category** (S2)—tools/DIY is not a fringe bet on paper.  
2. **Channel realism:** **Alza’s scale** (S3, S7) supports a **learn-fast** route: list a **tight SKU band**, measure conversion and margin after fees/shipping, then decide on owned-site investment.  
3. **Competitive gravity (the hard part):** **Temu at #2** (S3) means you must **avoid undifferentiated commodity SKUs**; win on **availability, warranty, compatibility content, and service**—exactly what Czech-language product depth (US2350) and trust UX should reinforce.

**What would make me change this verdict:**

- If pilot data shows **negative contribution margin** after marketplace commissions, returns, and cross-border logistics—even on “differentiated” SKUs—**pause** and shrink assortment (S5 suggests checkout/shipping/payment frictions are costly).  
- If **COD / pickup-network** expectations (common in CZ; ECDB flags COD prevalence at market level) clash with your operating model and cannot be mitigated, **reassess** payment and delivery design (S8, ECDB service-provider section on [ECDB Czechia](https://ecommercedb.com/markets/cz/all)).

## 4. Concrete next experiments (not theory)

1. **SKU shortlist (20–40):** pick models where you can beat Temu on **spec clarity** or **support**, not only price.  
2. **Price ladder scrape (manual):** for 10 SKUs, record **Heureka lowest offers** + **Alza buy box** + your landed cost → template margin table.  
3. **Operational readiness checklist:** Czech PDPs (US2350), Czech UI (US2300), PayU in checkout (US3100), delivery policy vs US2450 thresholds—**one** integrated demo path.  
4. **90-day KPI:** contribution margin after **refunds/returns**, not GMV.
