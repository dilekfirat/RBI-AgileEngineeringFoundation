# User Story: Define a Zero-Bug Policy

**Epic:** Quality Governance

## User Story

As a Head of Testing,

I want the Test Policy to define a Zero-Bug Policy,

so that software products are released based on transparent quality criteria, controlled risk decisions, and a shared understanding of defect management.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

### AC1 - Zero-Bug Policy Defined

The Test Policy defines the organization's Zero-Bug Policy and its objectives.

### AC2 - Defect Severity Classification Defined

The Test Policy defines defect severity levels and corresponding handling procedures.

### AC3 - Release Criteria Established

The Test Policy defines which defect severities are acceptable for release and which must be resolved before deployment.

### AC4 - Risk-Based Decisions Allowed

The Test Policy allows documented risk acceptance decisions by authorized stakeholders when defects cannot be resolved before release.

### AC5 - Defect Transparency Required

All known defects shall be documented, tracked, and visible to relevant stakeholders.

### AC6 - Root Cause Analysis Encouraged

The Test Policy requires significant defects to be analyzed to identify and address underlying causes.

### AC7 - Defect Prevention Included

The Test Policy promotes defect prevention practices such as reviews, test automation, exploratory testing, and shift-left testing.

### AC8 - Quality Ownership Defined

The Test Policy states that quality is a whole-team responsibility involving developers, testers, product owners, and business stakeholders.

### AC9 - Metrics Defined

The Test Policy defines quality metrics such as:

- Open defects by severity
- Defect leakage
- Mean time to resolution
- Escaped defects
- Technical debt indicators

### AC10 - Continuous Improvement Required

Defect trends and quality metrics are reviewed regularly to identify improvement opportunities.

---

## Policy Principles

- Every defect matters.
- No critical or blocker defects are released without explicit risk acceptance.
- Quality decisions are transparent and documented.
- Defect prevention is preferred over defect detection.
- Quality is a shared responsibility.
- Risk-based decision making is supported.

---

## Definition of Done

- Zero-Bug Policy section added to the Test Policy.
- Severity and release criteria defined.
- Defect management expectations documented.
- Risk acceptance process documented.
- Quality metrics defined.
- Continuous improvement principles included.
- Policy reviewed and approved by relevant stakeholders.
